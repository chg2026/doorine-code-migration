Gold Bridge Platform Blueprint.

A reference architecture for running CHG and Deal Link as two products under one parent account system — modeled on how Atlassian operates Loom, Trello, Jira, and Confluence under a single identity and org layer.

01 The question we're answering

One identity, many products, one funnel.

Deal Link is the free lead magnet. CHG is the paid enterprise tool. If they're two separate apps with two separate user bases, the funnel breaks. We need them to share identity, data, and reputation without sharing brand.
Option 1 — rejected

Shared DB, two backends

Two repos, two deploy pipelines, two sets of middleware. Same database underneath. Doubles infrastructure work for no user-facing gain.

Option 3 — rejected

SSO bridge between two apps

Federates login but not data. Reputation scores and deal records can't cleanly cross the boundary. Breaks the lead magnet.

Option 2 — chosen · Atlassian model

One account, many products

Single database, single API, single auth. Two separately-branded front-end apps. Users, billing, and reputation belong to a shared organization layer. The app switcher drives upsell.

02 What Atlassian is actually doing

Five layers, only one visible to users.

Observed from the chg2026 organization across Loom, Trello, and the Atlassian Admin console. The pattern is consistent across every Atlassian product and is the public blueprint we're mirroring.
Layer 1

Atlassian Account

The identity layer. One email, one password, one session — decoupled from any specific product. You log in once and every product you have access to is unlocked.
"My Atlassian account is the same whether I'm in Loom or Trello. The products don't own my identity."
Layer 2

Organization

The customer's company. Named chg2026 in the screenshots. Owns billing, directory, security policy, and product entitlements. One organization can have many products active in parallel.
Visible at admin.atlassian.com. This is where a customer's IT admin manages the whole estate — not inside any one product.
Layer 3

Products

Separately branded apps on separate domains — loom.com, trello.com, atlassian.net. Each product has its own plan tier per organization (Loom has a "Business-ai" plan independent of what Trello is on).
Products share identity and org, but keep their own UX, pricing, domain, and positioning. Trello is a brand. Loom is a brand. Atlassian is the parent.
Layer 4

Groups & Roles

Each product in each org gets its own groups: loom-admins-chg2026, loom-users-chg2026, loom-access-admins-chg2026. Three built-in roles — App admin, User access admin, User — scoped to that product only.
Being a Loom admin gives no authority in Trello. Cleanly separates who-can-do-what per product while keeping a unified user record.
Layer 5

App Switcher

The 3×3 grid icon in the top-left of every Atlassian product. Dropdown shows products the user already has access to, plus "Recommended for your team" — Jira Service Management, Confluence, etc. — which are the unpaid products the org could add.
The switcher is the upsell funnel. This is the mechanism that turns a Trello user into a Jira user. It's our lead magnet surface.
Invisible

Platform Super Admin

Atlassian-internal staff who can see and intervene across every organization in the system. Not exposed to customers. Sits above organization admins.
This is where Gold Bridge operations sits. Not the same as a customer's org admin.

06 Domain & routing strategy

Four domains, one session.

Atlassian keeps each product on its own brand domain — loom.com, trello.com, atlassian.net — and lets a shared session cookie carry identity between them. We do the same. The holding company's own site stays quiet.
Product · public + authed
deallink.io

The free lead-magnet product. Wholesaler-facing. Public profile pages live here (deallink.io/@handle). Own brand, own marketing, own URL.

Product · authed only
app.chg.io (or chg.com)

The paid enterprise CRM. Gated behind login. Confirm final domain in open decisions section.

Admin · authed only
admin.goldbridge.io

Unified admin console. Mirrors admin.atlassian.com. Used by both org admins (to manage their org) and platform super admins (to manage all orgs).

Corporate · silent
goldbridge.io

The parent holding company's presence. Low-traffic. Legal, investor relations, careers. Atlassian's equivalent is atlassian.com — you barely visit it unless you're researching the company.

Under the hood, one Express API server handles all four domains, routing by hostname. Supabase Auth issues a session cookie scoped to .goldbridge.io for all Gold Bridge surfaces; Deal Link runs on its own domain and coordinates session via a token exchange on first visit (same pattern Atlassian uses between Loom and the rest of the estate). One database. One source of truth.

07 Four levels of admin

Who can see what, and who they serve.

Today the codebase distinguishes two: super admin vs account admin. The Atlassian pattern needs four. This is the single biggest permissions change in the plan.
01
Platform tier · internal

Gold Bridge Super Admin

You and future platform engineering. Can see every organization, impersonate any user, force password resets, adjust any plan, investigate abuse, restore from backups. Invisible to customers.

Lives in admin.goldbridge.io under a separate "Platform" nav · is_super_admin() returns true
02
Customer tier · per-org

Organization Admin

The customer's own admin — typically the principal at a real-estate company. Manages their organization's directory, invites and removes users, controls billing, configures SSO, manages product entitlements. Can't see other organizations.

admin.goldbridge.io scoped to their org_id · existing isAccountAdmin flag
03
Customer tier · per-product

Product Admin

Manages a single product within an org. "CHG admin" can configure departments, approve contractor access, and adjust CHG-specific settings — but can't touch billing or the Deal Link side. Mirrors Atlassian's "App admin" role.

New role dimension · roles.product_id · permissions scoped to one product
04
End user

User

Ordinary user with product-scoped department roles. An acquisitions editor in CHG. A poster in Deal Link. Access is controlled by group membership and permission level (view / edit / admin) per department.

Existing hasDepartmentAccess() and canEditDepartment() extended to be product-aware

10 The lead magnet flow

Free to paid, without data migration.

Because Deal Link and CHG share one account record and one database, upgrading is a plan change — not a data migration. The wholesaler's deals, contacts, buyer relationships, and reputation carry over automatically.
Step 1

Sign up on Deal Link

Wholesaler lands at deallink.io, signs up with email. Supabase creates the auth user. Trigger creates an accounts row and an account_products entry: product = deallink, plan = free.

Step 2

Use the free product

Posts deals. Gets a public profile at deallink.io/@handle. Starts capturing buyer leads. Earns initial reputation reviews.

Step 3

Clicks CHG in switcher

Sees CHG in the "Recommended for your team" section of the app switcher. Clicks. Lands on a CHG trial CTA explaining portfolio management, construction tracking, full CRM.

Step 4

Starts CHG trial

One click adds a new row to account_products: product = chg, plan = trial, trial_ends_at = +14 days. Same account. Same user. No new signup flow.

Step 5

Converts to paid

Enters payment info. Plan changes to professional. Deal Link stays active on free. The wholesaler now runs both products from one login, with shared reputation and shared contact data.

11 Where reputation lives

One score, visible across products.

The trustability score attaches to the user, not to the product or the organization. It's written by Deal Link (wholesalers rate buyers, buyers rate wholesalers) and readable anywhere — on the public Deal Link profile, inside CHG's contact records, in the admin console for disputes.
Where it's stored
  • deallink_profiles.reputation_scoreAggregate, denormalized for read speed
  • deallink_profiles.review_countHow many reviews make up the score
  • reputation_reviews tableImmutable log of individual ratings
Where it's written
  • Deal Link — after a deal closesWholesaler rates buyer, buyer rates wholesaler
  • Deal Link — buyer drops interestSilent signal, not a rating but influences score
  • Admin console — dispute outcomesPlatform can adjust after review
Where it's read
  • Deal Link public profileVisible to anyone browsing wholesaler's page
  • CHG contact recordsShown when wholesaler reviews a buyer lead
  • Admin consolePlatform-wide for trust & safety work

13 Risk register

What could go wrong, and how we reduce it.

Not an exhaustive list — the things I think deserve conscious attention before phase 1 starts.
Risk
What happens
Severity
Mitigation
Schema migration breaks production
Adding product_id to roles and role_permissions is a structural change. If RLS policies reference the old shape, queries start returning nothing.
High
Rehearse on staging. Keep rollback script ready. Run during maintenance window. Keep CHG-as-default product assumption until all routes migrated.
Shared session across domains
Cookie-domain config for shared auth between goldbridge.io subdomains and deallink.io is non-trivial. Get this wrong, users see login walls mid-flow.
High
Use token-exchange pattern for the cross-domain hop (deallink.io exchanges a short-lived Supabase JWT on first visit). Test on staging subdomains before DNS goes live.
Two React apps drift over time
Deal Link and CHG share components, but if they live in separate repos the AppSwitcher, Topbar, and auth plumbing will diverge within months.
Medium
Monorepo from day one, or a published shared package. Same linter, same TypeScript config. Component library for anything cross-product.
Billing complexity
Per-product subscriptions means Stripe products per Gold Bridge product, plus proration when a customer adds or removes products mid-cycle.
Medium
Use Stripe's metered + subscription items model, not separate customer records. One Stripe customer per organization, many subscription items. Confirm with Stripe docs before phase 8.

Current signup rate limit uses an in-memory Map. Resets every deploy. Doesn't scale past one Replit instance.

14 Open decisions — need from Nicole

Nine answers before phase 1.

Not blocking for this blueprint, but blocking for implementation. My recommendation is listed under each — push back where you disagree.
01

CHG's final domain

Today it runs at chg-crm.replit.app. For production we need a real domain. Options: chg.com, chg.io, chgplatform.com, app.chg.io.

Recommendation: app.chg.io if you can secure chg.io; otherwise chg-platform.com.
02

Gold Bridge corporate domain

Is goldbridge.io available? Alternatives: goldbridgeholdings.com, goldbridgeco.com, goldbridgerei.com (already used for your email).

Recommendation: register goldbridge.io if available; fall back to goldbridgeco.com.
03

Deal Link's final domain

The wireframes say deallink.io. Confirm registered and available to us.

Recommendation: confirm ownership before we hardcode the domain anywhere.
04

Monorepo or three repos

Today chg-crm-main is one repo. Deal Link is wireframes, no repo yet. Admin console doesn't exist. Three apps, three repos — or one monorepo with packages.

Recommendation: monorepo. Easier shared components, one CI pipeline, one deploy story.
05

Supabase tier upgrade timing

Free tier has no backups. Pro tier ($25/mo) has 7-day daily + PITR. Team tier has 14-day + longer PITR window.

Recommendation: upgrade to Pro before phase 1 migration. You cannot run a schema migration on an un-backed-up production database.
06

Billing provider

Stripe is the default. Alternatives exist but come with friction. Needed for phase 8, decision earlier helps shape the products / plans schema.

Recommendation: Stripe, one customer per organization, metered subscription items per product.
07

Reputation score visibility

Public (on the Deal Link profile, visible to anyone)? Gated (visible only to logged-in users)? Asymmetric (wholesalers see all buyer scores, buyers only see wholesalers they've interacted with)?

Recommendation: public for wholesalers, gated for buyers. Wholesalers WANT their score visible — it's their credibility. Buyers would be uncomfortable.
08

Reputation dispute policy

If a user disputes a rating, does it auto-hide pending review? Or stay live with a "disputed" badge? Who reviews?

Recommendation: stays live with a small "disputed" indicator. Platform super admin reviews and either clears the dispute, removes the rating, or removes the rater (repeat offenders).
09

Gstack folder location

79 unrelated files sitting inside chg-crm-main/gstack/. Belongs in ~/.claude/skills/ or a separate toolkit repo — not shipped with the product.

Recommendation: move to ~/.claude/skills/ before phase 1. Lower CI time, cleaner repo for future agents.
End of blueprint

This document is a planning artifact, not an implementation contract. Every phase will generate its own tighter spec before coding starts. Nothing here is built yet. The point of this blueprint is alignment on direction — once confirmed, phase 0 begins.
